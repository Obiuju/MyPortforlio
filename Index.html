About

MScAnalytics — Northeastern University, Toronto

My research focuses on improving data quality in low- and middle-income countries (LMICs), particularly within health information systems and public health supply chains. I aim to explore the use of machine learning models to detect reporting anomalies, reduce data gaps, and enhance real-time data visibility in resource-constrained environments.

My emphasis is on developing scalable, interoperable solutions that are compatible with existing systems — with long-term goals of advancing context-aware frameworks for digital health systems.

Research

In development

Difficulty-Weighted Improvement Scoring (DWIS) Framework

Universal framework addressing flaws in equal-weight multi-dimensional evaluation using Pareto-based weighting. Validated on 9,591 assessments across 18 well-being domains. Cross-validation ongoing using the Multidimensional Poverty Index dataset.

Under peer review

AI and Big Data Analytics for Disease Surveillance in Nigeria

Systematic review of AI-enabled tools including SORMAS, ODK, and the National Data Repository. Proposed a scalable lightweight AI model for real-time outbreak detection and health system resilience.

Under peer review

Leveraging AI to Strengthen Data Integrity in Limited-Resource Settings

Review of health supply chain reporting challenges. Designed a lightweight AI model to prevent commodity stockouts and improve real-time reporting in resource-limited environments.

Article

Antimicrobial Resistance Surveillance — Lagos, Nigeria

Analyzed spatial and temporal antibiotic susceptibility data to detect multidrug-resistant Klebsiella pneumoniae patterns. Results supported early warning systems and regional public health response. Published on a private veterinary platform (aptvet.com, Jun 2023).
